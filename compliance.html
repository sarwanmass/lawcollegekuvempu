Academic & Welfare Committees

A state-of-the-art overview of statutory panels, cells, and clubs safeguarding student interests and driving quality assurance.

Anti-Ragging Committee

Mandated by the Hon'ble Supreme Court of India and UGC regulations to maintain a 100% ragging-free campus. Our dedicated anti-ragging squad ensures comprehensive student safety, dignity, and psychological peace.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Zero Tolerance Policy: Any verbal, written, or physical ragging triggers immediate rustication and FIR filing.
  • Anonymous Drop Boxes: Secured complaints can be dropped outside the Administrative Block.
  • Surprise Inspections: The squad conducts random check-ins at hostels, libraries, and surrounding cafes.
  • Nodal Officer: Dr. Gurumurthy T.N (Principal Desk) | murthyguru1987@gmail.com

SC/ST Grievance Cell

Established in strict accordance with the UGC guidelines to protect Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes students from discrimination, ensuring an inclusive, equitable academic environment.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Welfare Liaison: Oversees equal distribution of academic resources and coordinates state scholarships.
  • Remedial Coaching: Holds specialized, free mentoring classes to strengthen core subject competencies.
  • Expedited Inquiries: Ensures swift, objective adjudication of identity-based harassment claims.
  • Convenor: Dr. Muniraja M | munirajulc@gmail.com

Women's Grievance & ICC

Configured under the POSH Act (Prevention of Sexual Harassment), 2013, to provide a secure, empowering environment for female students, academic staff, and administrative members.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Confidentiality Guarantee: All proceedings, hearings, and counselors' dialogue are strictly private.
  • Gender Sensitization: Hosts guest legal seminars on equal rights, workplace safety, and consent.
  • CCTV & Escorts: Oversees functional campus lighting, security camera feeds, and campus wardens.
  • ICC Chairperson: Prof. Geetha H.D | geethahd26@gmail.com

Pro-Bono Legal Aid Clinic & Literacy Club

Fulfilling our constitutional mandate of social engineering. The Legal Aid Clinic provides critical, free legal counseling and rights litigation counseling to weaker sections of Basavanagudi and surrounding neighborhoods.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Community Camps: Students under professor supervision handle real legal inquiries from local residents.
  • Rights Literacy: Conducts street plays (Nukkad Nataks), pamphlets distribution, and talks on basic consumer rights.
  • Conciliation Forum: Aids in alternative dispute resolution and mediation counselling.
  • Director: Sunil M.V (Assistant Professor) | sunilmvmc@gmail.com

Moot Court Society (MCS)

The core practical advocacy hub of Rashtrakavi Kuvempu College of Law. The MCS simulates appellate-level high court and supreme court chambers, molding students into fearless trial litigators.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Weekly Mock Trials: Simulates civil, criminal, and constitutional hearings in our dedicated Moot Court Hall.
  • Drafting Clinics: Special workshops on drafting court briefs, memorials, petitions, and legal opinions.
  • National Representation: Prepares, finances, and coaches teams representing RKLC at national championships.
  • Faculty In-Charge: Dawood Baig K (Assistant Professor) | dawoodbaig.k@gmail.com

Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC)

A strategic statutory unit tasked with implementing NAAC-standard quality benchmarks, reviewing teaching methods, and overseeing continuous administrative enhancement.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Student Feedback loops: Drives quarterly feedback collections on teaching efficacy and library support.
  • Academic Audits: Periodic audits of syllabus coverage, internship journals, and research files.
  • Professional Seminars: Coordinates national conferences on constitutional law and human rights.
  • IQAC Director: Dr. Gurumurthy T.N | murthyguru1987@gmail.com

NSS Unit (National Service Scheme)

Nurturing civic empathy and communal responsibility. The NSS unit partners legal education with developmental service, engaging students in national development works.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Rural Camps: Coordinates annual 7-day developmental camps in adopted village communities.
  • Blood & Health Drives: Collaborates with Bengaluru hospitals for blood donations and eye care camps.
  • Ecological Action: Manages campus waste minimization and tree-planting initiatives in Nagasandra areas.
  • Program Officer: Mr. Shivakumar M | shivakumarpe@gmail.com

Grievance Redressal Cell

A democratic mechanism allowing students to voice, track, and resolve academic, infrastructure, and administrative concerns under fair-hearing protocols.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Tribunal Hearings: The cell meets fortnightly to review submitted grievances with student reps.
  • Objective Review: Grievances are assessed strictly based on institutional statutes and KSLU codes.
  • Transparency Reports: Publishes action-taken briefs for review by the Governing Council.
  • Convenor: Dr. Muniraja M | munirajulc@gmail.com

Library Committee

Guiding the operations, inventory growth, and digital asset acquisitions of the Law Library, ensuring resources for advanced legal research are robust.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Collection Audits: Advises on the subscription renewal of AIR, SCC, and legal research journals.
  • Book Bank Management: Oversees free core textbook lending setups for weaker section students.
  • Research Facilities: Directs layout expansions, Wi-Fi terminals, and quiet study room protocols.
  • Chief Librarian: Dr. Narayanaswamy B.V | narayanaswamybv@gmail.com

Sports & Cultural Committee

Balancing rigorous legal scholarship with creativity and fitness. This committee handles the sports meets, debates, and theater works that enrich college life.

Key Operations & Guidelines

  • Athletic Tournaments: Conducts internal cricket, chess, and athletic meets under PE oversight.
  • Debates & Drama: Hosts the annual legal theater festival simulating constitutional debates.
  • Wellness Camps: Organizes regular stress relief, yoga, and physical fitness programs.
  • Co-ordinators: Mrs. Sushmitha K (Cultural) & Mr. Shivakumar M (Sports)
